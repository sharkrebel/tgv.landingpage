# Business Glossary - Tam Giang Capital Domain

## Core Entity Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Tam Giang Capital (TGV)** | The investment arm and primary holding vehicle for the group's ecosystem. |
| **Ecosystem (Hệ Sinh Thái)** | The interconnected network of business verticals that share resources, technology, and strategic goals. |
| **Venture Vertical** | The branch focusing on financial investment, equity funding, and capital growth. |
| **Service Vertical** | Focuses on public service automation, primarily Social Insurance (BHXH) and Health Insurance (BHYT). |
| **Esport Vertical** | The digital entertainment arm, specializing in FC Online account services and community platforms. |
| **Manufacture Vertical** | High-tech agriculture and sustainable food production (Tam Giang Food). |

---

## Strategic Terms

| Term | Definition in TGV Context |
|------|---------------------------|
| **Cash Cow** | Stable, reliable revenue streams—specifically attributed to the Service vertical (BHXH/BHYT). |
| **Growth Vertical** | High-potential sectors capable of rapid scaling, specifically Esports and Venture branches. |
| **Bệ phóng (Launchpad)** | The concept of TGV providing the infrastructure for portfolio companies to accelerate growth. |
| **ESG Commitment** | Environmental, Social, and Governance standards, primarily applied to the Manufacture vertical for sustainable farming. |

---

## Technical Domain Language

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **BHXH (Bảo hiểm xã hội)** | Social Insurance program in Vietnam, a key focus of the Service vertical. |
| **BHYT (Bảo hiểm y tế)** | Health Insurance program, integrated into the site's online registration projects. |
| **FC Online** | The specific competitive gaming title (formerly FIFA Online) supported by the Esport vertical. |
| **Angel Investor** | The primary target avatar for the landing page's conversion efforts. |
| **Landing Page Portal** | The architecture used to bridge multiple distinct web properties into a single group identity. |
